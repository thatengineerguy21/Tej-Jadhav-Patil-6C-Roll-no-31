Ila Sachani - An Inspirational Journey

About Ila Sachani

Ila Sachani is an inspiring figure from Moti Vavdi village in Bhavnagar, Gujarat. Despite being born with a condition that left her hands unable to function normally, she learned to embroider using her feet under the guidance of her mother and grandmother, who were skilled in Kathiawar embroidery.

Early Life and Challenges

Ila Sachani was born with a condition that affected the functionality of her hands. Despite this challenge, she was determined to lead a normal life and contribute to her family. Her mother and grandmother, both proficient in Kathiawar embroidery, decided to teach her the art using her feet.

Learning and Mastering Embroidery

Learning to embroider with her feet was no easy task for Ila. She had to practice threading needles and mastering various embroidery styles. Through unwavering determination and family support, she became proficient in Kathiawar embroidery and learned other styles like Kachhi, Kashmiri, and Lucknawi embroidery.

Specific Embroidery Techniques

Ila specializes in various traditional embroidery techniques, including:

  • Kathiawar Embroidery: Known for its vibrant colours and intricate patterns, this style is native to the Kathiawar region in Gujarat.
  • Kachhi Embroidery: Characterized by mirror work and vibrant threads, this style is popular in the Kutch region of Gujarat.
  • Kashmiri Embroidery: Featuring delicate floral patterns, this style is native to the Kashmir region and is known for its elegance and beauty.
  • Lucknawi Embroidery: Also known as Chikankari, this style originates from Lucknow and is famous for its fine, intricate needlework on fabric.
