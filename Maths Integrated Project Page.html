Contributions of Indian Mathematicians in Maths

Srinivasa Ramanujan

Srinivasa Ramanujan was a mathematical genius whose contributions include the Ramanujan prime, the Ramanujan theta function, partition formulae, and mock theta functions. His work continues to inspire mathematicians around the world.

  • Ramanujan's Theta Function: A special function in mathematics with applications in number theory.
  • Ramanujan's Prime: A special type of prime number.
  • Mock Theta Functions: Functions in mathematics that are similar to modular forms but do not meet all the requirements of modular forms.
  • Partition Formulae: Formulas related to partitioning numbers into sums of smaller numbers.

Brahmagupta

Brahmagupta was a 7th-century Indian mathematician and astronomer. His works influenced future generations of mathematicians and scholars. Key contributions include:

  • Rules for Solving Quadratic Equations: Including negative solutions.
  • Zero and its Properties: One of the first to discuss the concept and properties of zero in detail.
  • Brahmagupta’s Identity: An identity in number theory.
  • Formula for Area of a Cyclic Quadrilateral: A special case of Brahmagupta's formula.
  • Methods for Solving Pell's Equation: Important in number theory.

Aryabhata

Aryabhata was one of the first mathematicians to discuss the heliocentric model, approximations for pi, and trigonometric functions. Key contributions include:

  • Approximation of Pi: Aryabhata provided an approximation of pi (π) which was remarkably accurate for his time.
  • Place Value System and Zero: Contributions to the development of the place value system and the use of zero.
  • Aryabhata's Algorithm: An algorithm for solving Diophantine equations.
  • Astronomical Calculations: His work included calculating the length of the year and the earth's rotation.

Bhaskara II (Bhaskaracharya)

Bhaskara II was a 12th-century mathematician whose contributions to algebra, trigonometry, and calculus were ahead of his time. Important works include:

  • Lilavati: A book on arithmetic which is also an elegant piece of literature.
  • Bijaganita: His work on algebra.
  • Spherical Trigonometry: Introduction and contributions to spherical trigonometry.
  • Calculus: Early concepts and techniques in differential and integral calculus.
  • Astronomy: Contributions to the field of astronomy.

C.R. Rao

Calyampudi Radhakrishna Rao, known as C.R. Rao, is a prominent contemporary statistician. Noteworthy contributions include:

  • Cramér-Rao Bound: A fundamental theorem in statistics.
  • Rao-Blackwell Theorem: A method of improving estimators.
  • Rao Distance: A concept in statistical shape analysis.
  • Contributions to Multivariate Analysis: Significant contributions to the field of multivariate statistics.
